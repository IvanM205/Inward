# ADR-003 — Open Hand payments: platform IAP first

- **Status:** Proposed
- **Date:** 2026-06-12
- **Deciders:** Ivan Marianek (product owner) — pending; drafted by Claude (build agent)

## Context

The Open Hand (OPEN-01..05) takes voluntary contributions (€3 / €8 / €15 /
custom), monthly at most, with the app identical for payers and non-payers
(INV-8). `05-features.md §OPEN-05` delegates the rails: platform in-app
purchase or an external PSP. No payment data may live on the device beyond
receipts; the anonymous supporter count (OPEN-04) is the only counter.

## Options

### A — External PSP (Stripe or similar)

- For: smaller fees (~3% vs 15–30%), one implementation for both platforms,
  contributions possible outside the stores' rules for "donations".
- Against: requires a checkout webview or browser hop (a jarring exit from a
  terminal-screen product); the PSP sees an identity (card data) that the
  product otherwise never has; store policies around digital-goods payments
  remain a compliance risk for an app *sold through those stores*.

### B — Platform IAP (proposed for v1)

Consumable IAP products at the three fixed amounts plus a small set of
custom steps; "restore" is meaningless by design (nothing is unlocked).
- For: native sheet keeps the flow quiet and on-screen; Apple/Google handle
  identity and receipts so Inward continues to hold nothing (NFR-P1); store
  compliance is unambiguous.
- Against: 15–30% fee on gifts — accepted for v1 as the cost of the quietest
  honest flow; revisit post-launch (a PSP path can be added for the web/
  printed-book channels later without touching the app's covenant).

## Decision (proposed)

Option B for v1. The supporter count (OPEN-04) is incremented by a
store-server webhook into the same tiny service that serves the count — the
device never reports a purchase anywhere except to the store itself.

## Covenant test

- INV-8: contributions unlock nothing; the IAP products are consumables with
  no entitlement attached. PASS.
- OPEN-02/03: rails don't touch the ask rules — those are enforced on-device
  (src/app/openhand/openHandRepo.ts) regardless of processor. PASS.
- NFR-P1: no new user-data endpoint; the count stays a single integer. PASS.

## Consequences

M4 wires AskFlow's amount actions to a PaymentSheet port; the current
markContributed-on-tap behavior becomes markContributed-on-receipt. This ADR
awaits the owner's acceptance before any store configuration.
